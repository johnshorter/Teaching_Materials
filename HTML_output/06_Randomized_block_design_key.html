The researcher was testing the effect of different pastures (P1 to P5) on the amount of beta-carotene in egg. There were 4 fields (F1 - F4). The fields are the blocking factors. In each field, hens were randomly assigned to be fed with one of the 5 pastures. At the end of the experiment, eggs were collected and beta-carotene concentration was assayed. (Data from UC Davis Plant Sciences course PLS205, Winter 2017)

make the best plot you can to visualize the data.

Setting up the model

Fill out the table:

What are the sources of variation? What factors are they? (treatment? block? observational units? experimental units?) How many levels in each?

source factor levels
Pature treatment 5
Field block 4
Hen EU 20

What should you include in the model? Set up the linear model.

model_beta <- lm(Beta ~ Pasture + Field, data = chicken_experiment)

Check assumptions of ANOVA

plot(model_beta, which = c(1, 2, 3))

Normality? Pretty good. Equal variance? There might be a bit more variance at the higher end of fitted values. You can try a log transformation, but I don’t think it is very critical here.

Run Tukey tests and show cld

estimate_beta <- emmeans(model_beta, pairwise ~ Pasture)
